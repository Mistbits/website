About Mistbits / South Africa

Built close to
the problem.

We are a small IoT team that likes hard, useful problems—especially the ones involving water, remote infrastructure, and technology that has to keep working outside the lab.

Where we come from

Hardware / firmware
cloud / applications

Mistbits grew out of a practical need: make remote water infrastructure easier to see, understand, and operate.

One of our longest-running collaborations is Smarta, a borehole and reservoir monitoring platform built alongside a South African water company. From the early field units onward, Mistbits has worked across the custom hardware, firmware, backend, dashboards, reports, and mobile app. The two businesses have grown alongside each other.

That work shaped how we build. We prefer direct contact with operators, short feedback loops, and systems we can understand from sensor input to screen. Most of what we make is custom-built and handled in-house, so the device and software can evolve as one system.

Water remains at the centre of our work today. Alongside pump and reservoir operations, we are building tools for automatic estate water-meter readings, leak visibility, consumption analytics, and billing workflows.

What guides us

Useful beats
impressive.

Remote systems reward clear thinking. Our principles are simple because the environments we work in are complicated enough.

01

Reliability over novelty

We care more about uptime, understandable behaviour, and easy recovery than whatever happens to be fashionable.

02

The whole path matters

A good dashboard cannot rescue bad field data. We design the sensor, connection, backend, and interface as one path.

03

Stay close to operators

The people using the system know where it helps and where it gets in the way. We build with that feedback nearby.

04

Make the data actionable

Telemetry should answer a question, raise the right alarm, trigger useful action, or make the next decision clearer.

Looking ahead

A quieter kind
of network.

The Mistbits Whisper Network is a Meshtastic®-based foundation we are exploring for low-power IoT deployments in remote and rural places. It is infrastructure rather than a packaged product: a place for edge-connected devices, peer-to-peer tools, and open protocols to meet real field needs.
